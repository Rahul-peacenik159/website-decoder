# Design + Illustration Decode Prompt

Use this when you want to understand HOW illustrations are designed, why they work, and how to apply the same thinking to your own visuals.

Attach the full analysis report and say:

---

## Prompt

You are a senior product designer and brand illustrator. I've shared a structured analysis of a website including screenshots, color palette, and SVG composition data. Break down how the illustrations and visual design are built — and why they work.

---

### 01 Illustration Style Breakdown

- What is the core illustration style? (flat, isometric, line art, 3D, character-based, abstract, data-viz, geometric)
- Are illustrations custom or from a library? What signals suggest this?
- How detailed are the illustrations — highly detailed, medium fidelity, or intentionally sparse/minimal?
- Are they representational (show the product/feature literally) or metaphorical (abstract the concept)?

---

### 02 Color Usage in Illustrations

- What is the color palette within the illustrations? How many colors are used?
- How does the illustration palette relate to the site's brand colors? Do they match exactly, complement, or contrast?
- Are illustrations monochromatic (one color family, varying opacity) or multi-color?
- Is opacity layering used to create depth without adding more colors?
- What technique creates the "soft" or "glassy" look if present? (low opacity fills, blur, gradients?)

---

### 03 How Illustrations Relate to Content

- Do illustrations explain the feature/concept they sit next to, or are they decorative?
- Are illustrations used to simplify complex technical ideas? How?
- Is there a visual metaphor connecting the illustration to the headline copy?
- Do illustrations tell a story across the page (sequential narrative) or are they standalone?
- Which comes first visually — the illustration or the text? What does that hierarchy signal?

---

### 04 Composition & Layout

- How are illustrations positioned relative to text? (left-right split, above text, behind text, overlapping)
- Are illustrations full-bleed, contained in cards, or floating freely?
- How much whitespace surrounds the illustrations?
- Are there layered depth effects (elements at different z-levels)?
- Are illustrations used as section dividers or background elements?

---

### 05 SVG Illustration Construction (Technical Design)

Based on the SVG breakdown:
- How are the SVGs structured? (single flat paths, grouped layers, symbol reuse)
- Are they built for animation from the start? (separate groups, named layers, clean path construction)
- What drawing technique is used? (bezier paths, geometric shapes combined, stroke-based vs fill-based)
- How are shadows and depth created in a flat SVG? (subtle offset shapes, layered opacity)

---

### 06 Motion Design Principles

- How does illustration animation add meaning vs just decoration?
- Are animated elements drawing attention to key information?
- Does the animation have a clear "resting state" and "excited state"?
- What is the animation's visual weight — does it feel heavy/dramatic or light/subtle?
- Is animation used to show cause and effect (e.g. input → output, click → result)?

---

### 07 Design–Copy Harmony

- Does the illustration visually reinforce the headline message?
- Is there a consistent metaphor system across the whole page? (e.g. everything uses a "flow" metaphor)
- Are icons and small illustrations consistent in style with larger hero illustrations?
- How does color transition between light and dark sections? Is the illustration style adapted?

---

### 08 What Makes the Illustration System Work

- What are the 3 design decisions that make this illustration system feel cohesive?
- What would happen if they removed the illustrations — would the page still communicate effectively?
- What's the relationship between illustration complexity and the surrounding content density?

---

### 09 How to Build a Similar System

If I were building my own illustration system to match this style:

1. **Style definition** — what rules would I write for an illustrator or for prompting AI image tools?
2. **Color system** — how many colors, what roles (background, line, highlight, accent)?
3. **Animation rules** — what moves, what stays still, and what are the timing principles?
4. **Tools to use** — Figma, Illustrator, SVG code, Rive, or other?
5. **Minimum viable illustration set** — what would I build first to get 80% of the visual impact?

---

### 10 One Design Principle This Site Nails

Identify the single most elegant or clever design decision on this site — the one thing that's not obvious but makes everything feel more premium, trustworthy, or memorable. Explain why it works.
