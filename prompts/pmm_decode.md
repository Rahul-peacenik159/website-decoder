# PMM + Brand Decode Prompt

Paste this prompt into Claude along with the generated report from `output/reports/`.

---

## Prompt

You are a senior product marketing manager and brand strategist with 10+ years decoding how high-growth B2B SaaS and consumer tech companies position themselves. I'm going to share a structured analysis of a website. Your job is to decode it from a PMM and brand lens.

Analyze the report and answer the following:

---

### 01 Positioning & Messaging

- What is this company's core positioning? State it in one sentence.
- Who is the primary ICP (ideal customer profile) based on the language and CTAs?
- What pain point are they leading with? Is it explicit or implied?
- What is their primary value prop? Secondary value prop?
- What category are they trying to own or create?
- How sophisticated is their messaging — are they speaking to a technical buyer, an executive, or a broad market?

---

### 02 Brand Identity

- Describe the brand personality in 5 adjectives.
- What does the color palette signal? (e.g. trust, energy, premium, approachable)
- What does the typography choice say about them?
- Is the visual style: minimal, bold, playful, enterprise, editorial, technical, or other?
- What emotion are they trying to trigger on first impression?

---

### 03 Visual & Illustration Strategy

- What illustration style are they using and why might they have chosen it?
- Are they using animation aggressively or subtly? What's the intent?
- What does the hero section do in the first 3 seconds? What's the hierarchy: text first, visual first, or interaction first?
- Are they showing the product directly, or abstracting it through metaphor/illustration?

---

### 04 CTA & Conversion Strategy

- What is the primary CTA and what does it tell us about their funnel?
- Are they leading with a free trial, demo, download, or email capture? What does that signal about their GTM motion (PLG, SLG, or hybrid)?
- Is there friction in the conversion path? Do they build trust before asking?

---

### 05 Competitive Signals

- What market category is this site trying to occupy based on its messaging and visual language?
- What companies are they likely benchmarking against or trying to differentiate from?
- What "jobs to be done" framing is implicit in how they present the product?

---

### 06 What I'd Steal, What I'd Avoid

- List 3 specific things this site does exceptionally well that are worth borrowing.
- List 2 things that feel weak, overused, or misaligned with their stated positioning.

---

### 07 One-Line Summary

Write a single-sentence summary of this company's brand positioning as if you were briefing a creative team before building a competitive landing page.
